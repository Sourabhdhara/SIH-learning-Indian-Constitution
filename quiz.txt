How many fundamental rights are guaranteed by the Indian Constitution?
A. 5
B. 6
C. 7
D. 8
Correct: B
Explanation: The Indian Constitution guarantees 6 fundamental rights: Right to Equality, Right to Freedom, Right against Exploitation, Right to Freedom of Religion, Cultural and Educational Rights, and Right to Constitutional Remedies.

---

Which article is known as the 'Heart and Soul' of the Constitution?
A. Article 21
B. Article 32
C. Article 14
D. Article 19
Correct: B
Explanation: Article 32 (Right to Constitutional Remedies) is known as the 'Heart and Soul' of the Constitution because it provides the right to approach the Supreme Court for enforcement of fundamental rights.

---

When did the Indian Constitution come into effect?
A. 26 January 1949
B. 26 January 1950
C. 15 August 1947
D. 2 October 1950
Correct: B
Explanation: The Indian Constitution came into effect on 26 January 1950, which is celebrated as Republic Day.

Who is known as the 'Father of the Indian Constitution'?
A. Mahatma Gandhi
B. Jawaharlal Nehru
C. Dr. B.R. Ambedkar
D. Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel
Correct: C
Explanation: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar is known as the 'Father of the Indian Constitution' as he was the Chairman of the Drafting Committee.

---

Which part of the Constitution deals with Fundamental Rights?
A. Part II
B. Part III
C. Part IV
D. Part V
Correct: B
Explanation: Part III of the Indian Constitution deals with Fundamental Rights (Articles 12-35).

---

How many schedules are there in the Indian Constitution?
A. 10
B. 11
C. 12
D. 13
Correct: C
Explanation: There are 12 schedules in the Indian Constitution covering various lists and provisions.

Which amendment is known as the 'Mini Constitution'?
A. 42nd Amendment
B. 44th Amendment
C. 73rd Amendment
D. 1st Amendment
Correct: A
Explanation: The 42nd Amendment (1976) is called the 'Mini Constitution' due to the extensive changes it made.

What does Article 21 guarantee?
A. Right to Equality
B. Right to Life and Personal Liberty
C. Right to Freedom of Religion
D. Right to Property
Correct: B
Explanation: Article 21 guarantees the Right to Life and Personal Liberty, which is one of the most important fundamental rights.

Which house is known as the 'House of Elders'?
A. Lok Sabha
B. Rajya Sabha
C. Vidhan Sabha
D. Vidhan Parishad

Explanation: Rajya Sabha is known as the 'House of Elders' or the Upper House of Parliament.
